At the first stall, she purchased leather oil that she needed to rework her quiver. The lady at the end of the merchants’ stalls carried bread, dried meat, and some nuts for Nevermore. Raven chuckled at the merchant’s quizzical stare. If only she knew who was helping me eat this. Tucking away her purchases, she hurried to the edge of the fort. She had left the animals alone long enough, and it was time to get moving again.

Once she reached the animals, she gave each of them some lunch, and grabbed a bite of bread. “Let’s get going, guys. We have a lot of ground to cover before sundown.” The afternoon passed quickly as Raven walked along talking to her pets and enjoying the sunshine. Suddenly, Nevermore shrieked in warning, and Raven notched an arrow immediately, searching for the danger. Even Sparklefly sat perfectly still, a low growl coming from his throat.
