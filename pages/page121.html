Trauma rose the next morning much the way he had the morning before. The sunlight was not his friend, and he made sure that it was understood between them. He saw the barkeep as he descended the stairs, the smell of coffee hitting his nose. Without a word, the barkeep placed a mug of steaming black coffee on the bar, and walked away. Trauma enjoyed the coffee, left a tip, and strode out of the inn determined to find his prey.
