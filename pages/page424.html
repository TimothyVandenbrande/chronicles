Alex held up his torch and once again blew the flame. A wave of fire spewed forward, engulfing the mordrem. Screaming in agony, and begging Mordremoth for help, the mobs fell to the ground, and quickly burned to ashes. Proud of himself, Alex turned to Raven with a grin. “Great idea, Alex! You can be point for now, and make crispy critters out of these things!” she exclaimed. Nodding his agreement, Alex charged around the next curve in the path. More screams could be heard as Alex made short work of the mobs. Catching up to him, the others saw that the trail forked once more. The right branch seemed to climb, while the left one wound down to what looked like more water.

“Not that I like getting wet, but I say we go left,” Raven said as they studied the road.

“I agree with Raven,” Vayne chimed in. “I don’t think we need to go up. I think that would take us in the wrong direction.”
