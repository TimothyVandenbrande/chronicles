Landing square in the middle, she closed her glider, and walked back to the edge to wait for the others. One by one the others jumped for the updraft and rode the lift to the island. After they had all landed, they spread out and explored the island. There was nothing on the island. It was a large, flat rock—no plants, no animals, no nothing. From where they stood, all the surrounding islands looked the same.

“I’m kinda disappointed,” Vayne said. “Mordremoth’s lair is very sparsely furnished.”

“Indeed,” Trauma replied. “Speaking of which. I wonder where our host is. Surely, he didn’t run out to buy drinks knowing we were coming. That would be bad form. Tisk, tisk.”
