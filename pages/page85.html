They ate in silence, and Tarr was the first to speak.

“I understand that you have a problem, and need help. Boar’s message was very cryptic, but I can understand why if it is a sensitive matter,” the charr said. His voice was gravelly, but not harsh, and his eyes carried a certain amount of wisdom that Raven could appreciate.

“Yes, it is a bit of a sensitive matter,” Raven said as she checked her surroundings for eavesdroppers. “I don’t want to go into the details here, but we have a big critter that needs taken care of and Boar said that you could help us since he is too far away studying.”

“That I can. My studies of the oozes in the cave are finished for now. I can go through my research anywhere. I cannot however, speak for my companion here,” Tarr indicated the small asura beside him. Raven turned to fully look at the asura, and noticed that he was twitchy like most of his kind, but seemed pleasant enough.
