“Slow down,” Vayne said as she steadied herself. “I’m only staying the night, maybe tomorrow at the most. I’m on business, not pleasure.”

Petra’s face fell a little, but then she quickly recovered. “Doesn’t matter. As long as you are here. Are you hungry?”

Suddenly realizing just how tired and hungry she was, Vayne nodded her head. Petra went behind the bar and fetched Vayne some stew and bread, and a big pint of ale. Motioning Vayne to follow, Petra set the tray down on a small table tucked under the stairs near the fire. Once they were seated, Petra filled Vayne in on all the latest gossip as she ate. Chiming in here and there, Vayne mainly let her talk, using the time to rest and gather her thoughts. Wiping her mouth, Vayne pushed her tray forward and sat back.
